Skateistan began as a grassroots 'Sport for Development' project on the streets of Kabul in 2007.
Skateistan is the first international development initiative to combine skateboarding with educational outcomes.
Skateistan use skateboarding as a tool for empowering youth, to create new opportunities and the potential for change.
Oliver Percovich dropped his board in Kabul in 2007, he was surrounded by the eager faces of children of all ages who wanted to be shown how to skate.
A group of Afghan friends (aged 18-22) who were naturals at skateboarding shared the three boards and quickly progressed in their new favourite sport.
Skateistan believes that when youth come together to skateboard and play, they forge bonds that transcend social barriers.

Thalente (Talent) Biyela is a 16 year old charismatic street kid who roams around Durban’s beach front. His charisma and charm has a way of drawing people towards him. His mom named him accordingly, Thalente, which means Talent in Zulu. He is an aspiring young skater who has hopes of becoming Pro. Thalente has been on and off the streets since the tender age of 8 years old. He has spent the last 4 years full-time on the streets hassling his way around the South African skate scene while trying to make a name for himself. He is motivated to use his skating talent as a tool to get him off the streets and make a career for himself. His skating is his life and he puts his heart and soul in to the sport. In order to pursue his dreams he needs OUR help were we can lend a hand. If anyone is interested in helping him please visit the “how you can help” section on this blog!
